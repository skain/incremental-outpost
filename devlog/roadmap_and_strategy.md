# Devlog Roadmap & Strategy

## Project Overview
**Project Name:** Incremental Outpost
(A Godot-based game project currently in active development.)

## The Narrative Hook (The "Twist")
This devlog chronicles the journey of an **experienced web programmer making their first game.** 

*Context:* While you have a deep background in structured coding and building complex systems for the web, the transition to gamedev introduces a new set of hurdles: moving from \"functional tools\" to \"engaging experiences.\" The series will highlight this shift—the application of seasoned engineering logic to the nuances of game-feel, shaders, state-driven art, and visceral feedback.

## Devlog Mission Statement
Create a series of YouTube devlog episodes that chronologically document the growth of 'Incremental Outpost'. The goal is to capture the transformation from a core mechanical prototype into a polished, atmospheric experience.

## Chronological Development Stages (The Roadmap)
Instead of categorizing by "Type", we will track progress through specific **Eras**:

**1. Phase I: The Groundwork (The Prototype Era)**
*   Focus: Establishing the solid foundations of common game loops.
*   Narrative Focus: Transitioning from technical requirements to interactive feedback. Getting things move, collide, and react. 
*   Content Highlights: Projectiles, basic movement logic, core impact systems, and initial "proof-of-concept" milestones.

**2. Phase II: The Expansion (The System Growth Era)**
*   Focus:* Expanding your scope from single mechanics to complex interconnected systems.
*   Narrative Focus:* Engineering the "Game Economy." Moving from a playable prototype to an evolving game world. 
*   Content Highlights:* Skill Tree implementation, advanced UI management, and deeper progression logic.

**3. Phase III: The Transformation (The Polish Era)**
*   Focus:** Bridging the gap between "works correctly" and \"feels magical.\"
*   Narrative Focus:* Mastery of aesthetic polish. This is where high-impact items (Shaders, layered soundscapes, muzzle flashes) create the final product's atmosphere.
*   Content Highlights:* CRT Shader integration, SFX layering/bus handling, and advanced particle effects.

## Content Mapping Methodology
*   **Chronology Check:** Use git logs to identify exactly where these "eras" shift in your history (e.g., noticing the jump when you moved from core mechanics to UI polish).
*   **The Bridge:** Highlight instances where your web-dev expertise helped solve a hard problem, but a \"game developer moment\" was required to make it feel right for the player.
*   **Growth Tracking:** Proving how much progress was made between "Day 1" and now by comparing older commits with recent ones.
